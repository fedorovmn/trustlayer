Security reports are easy to share. Hard to verify.

Today, most audit and compliance evidence is exchanged as files and screenshots. Once shared, it becomes difficult for external stakeholders to verify whether the document is original, current, or unchanged.

01

Too much trust in static files

PDFs and exported reports are easy to forward, rename, duplicate, or modify outside the original audit workflow.

02

Vendor reviews take longer

Security and procurement teams often need extra back-and-forth before accepting an audit as reliable.

03

No independent proof layer

Organizations lack a simple, universal way to prove that an audit result is authentic and unchanged.

A cleaner trust model for audit verification.

TrustLayer records a cryptographic fingerprint of an audit report, anchors that proof on-chain, and provides a simple verification interface for external stakeholders.

How it works

TrustLayer fits into an existing audit workflow without replacing the auditor, the report, or the delivery process.

1

Finalize the audit report

The completed report is uploaded by an authorized party after the audit is ready to share.

2

Generate a cryptographic fingerprint

TrustLayer creates a hash that uniquely represents the exact contents of that file.

3

Record proof on-chain

The fingerprint is anchored to a blockchain network such as Polygon to establish immutable proof.

4

Verify from anywhere

Clients and partners can validate the integrity of the report through a verification page or API call.
